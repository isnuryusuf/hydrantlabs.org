picoCTF 2013: Classic (70)

Challenge:

Someone or something stuck a random flyer on your space ship that reads:

cslcehesehft ohrumvc zmvm scmk ht ptohmte ehbmc mxmt eufsju eumq pvm dshem mpchgq lvfymt zheu nsce p rmtohg ptk rprmv, fv bfvm vmomtegq lq ofbbft ofbrsemvhwmk effgc. qfsv ymq hc: zumt_kf_zm_jme_ef_eum_upvk_cesii

Solution:

A quick look at the ciphertext it is clear this a simple substitution cipher. While one could certainly go about breaking this manually, there is no reason not to use the power of computers. I threw the ciphertext into Decrypto and a few seconds later I got the plaintext:

substitution ciphers were used in ancient times even though they are quite easily broken with just a pencil and paper, or more recently by common computerized tools. your key is: when_do_we_get_to_the_hard_stuff

Clearly the final key is:

when_do_we_get_to_the_hard_stuff
